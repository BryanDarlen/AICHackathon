## Introduction

Welcome to **AI Marathon 2026**.

Organized by the Artificial Intelligence Club (AIC) at Asia Pacific University (APU), AI Marathon is designed for students who are interested in exploring the world of Artificial Intelligence. With the theme **LLM Everywhere**, the event highlights how Large Language Models (LLMs) are transforming industries and creating new opportunities for innovation.

Throughout the AI Marathon, participants will take part in expert talks, hands-on workshops, interactive sessions, and a hackathon challenge where teams work together to develop creative AI solutions for real-world problems.

Whether you are new to AI or already experienced, this event is an opportunity to learn, collaborate, and build something meaningful together.

## Event Overview

AI Marathon 2026 is a multi-day technology event that brings together students, industry experts, and AI enthusiasts to explore practical applications of Artificial Intelligence.

Participants will attend expert talks, join hands-on technical workshops, and take part in interactive sessions designed to build both conceptual understanding and practical skills.

The highlight of the event is the **Hackathon Challenge**, where participants work in teams to design innovative AI solutions for real-world problems. Finalist teams will present their solutions during the final pitching session.

## Hackathon Challenge

The Hackathon Challenge is the core experiential component of AI Marathon. Participants will apply knowledge gained from talks and workshops to develop innovative solutions using LLMs and agentic AI.

Participants will collaborate in teams to design, prototype, and refine AI-powered solutions within a limited development period. The problem statements will be revealed during the event and will reflect current industry trends and real-world applications of LLM technologies.

At the end of the hackathon, all teams will submit their projects for evaluation. Top-performing teams will be selected as finalists and advance to the final pitching session.

## Rules and Regulations

### Eligibility

- Open to students from any university who have registered for AI Marathon.
- Participation in talks and workshops is free.
- Workshops are intermediate-level and require basic programming knowledge.
- Talks are beginner-friendly.
- Hackathon participation is optional and requires separate registration with a designated fee.
- Teams may include members from different institutions.

### Participation

- Teams may consist of 1 to 4 members.
- Solo participation is welcome.
- Participants may choose their level of involvement in talks, workshops, or the hackathon.
- Teams must register before the deadline.
- Team changes after registration are not permitted.
- All participants are expected to follow the event code of conduct.
- Harassment, discrimination, or disruptive behaviour may result in disqualification.

### AI Usage Requirement

- All submissions must incorporate LLMs and agentic AI.
- AI must be used meaningfully to enhance functionality, efficiency, or impact.

### Technical Stack

- Teams may use any tools, frameworks, or APIs relevant to their solution.
- Effective integration of AI and other technologies will be highly valued.

### Awards

- All prizes must be shared among team members according to their mutual agreement.
- Prizes are non-transferable and may only be claimed by the designated winners.
- The decisions of the judges and organizers are final and binding.

### Liability

- Organizers reserve the right to modify rules without prior notice.
- Participants assume all risks related to participation.
- Organizers are not responsible for any damage, loss, or injuries.

### Data Protection and Privacy

Participant information will be kept secure and used only for event purposes unless consent is given.

### Intellectual Property

Participants retain intellectual property rights to their projects. Teams grant organizers a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to showcase and promote submitted projects for marketing and educational purposes.

## Judging Criteria

| Category | Weight | Sub-Criteria | Points |
| --- | ---: | --- | ---: |
| Impact and Problem Relevance | 45% | Track Understanding and Completeness | 20 |
| Impact and Problem Relevance | 45% | Solution Effectiveness | 15 |
| Impact and Problem Relevance | 45% | Scalability and Future Potential | 10 |
| Innovation and Creativity | 30% | Originality of Idea | 20 |
| Innovation and Creativity | 30% | Tool Use and AI Orchestration | 10 |
| Technical Implementation | 25% | Agentic Architecture and Design | 15 |
| Technical Implementation | 25% | Prototype Functionality | 10 |

### Evaluation Focus

- Does the team demonstrate a clear understanding of the chosen track?
- Does the solution cover the main tasks requested in the problem statement?
- Does the agent produce meaningful outputs?
- Is the approach logical, well-justified, and practical?
- Can the solution be scaled beyond the hackathon prototype?
- Are external tools integrated creatively and effectively?
- Is the agentic architecture clear and well-structured?
- Does the prototype demonstrate the core functionality of the solution?

## Submission Guidelines

- The submitted solution must reflect the team's original work completed during the hackathon duration.
- Solutions must use LLM or AI technology to address the selected problem statement.
- Plagiarism of ideas, code, or designs is strictly prohibited.
- Submissions must be made before the deadline.
- Incomplete, illegible, or late submissions will not be considered.
- Submissions containing offensive, illegal, or unethical content will result in immediate disqualification.

### Phase 1: Preliminary Round Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Pitch Deck | Maximum 10 slides, excluding title and thank-you slides. Must outline the problem statement, solution architecture, business value, and team composition. |
| Agent Framework Diagram | Must be included directly within the pitch deck and clearly visualize the agent workflow. |
| Video Demo | 3-4 minute recorded walkthrough of the working solution. Focus on the user experience and underlying AI capabilities. |
| Source Code and Documentation | Link to a public or shared repository with a proper README.md file. The README must include system requirements, dependencies, and step-by-step instructions to install, configure, and run the application locally. |

### Phase 2: Grand Final Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Enhanced Project | Refined source code incorporating feedback from the preliminary round. |
| Final Pitch Deck | Updated and polished pitch deck optimized for a live presentation to the grand panel of judges. |
